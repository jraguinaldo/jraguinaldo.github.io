We're proud, don't overthink (Goal and Solutions)

The Philippines was definitely known for its exposure of pride. This exposure is what most young people on social media want. This exposure even makes the most extrovert of extroverts jealous. This exposure is like having a really obvious sunburn. This exposure is like showing how much of a fraud they are. All I’m saying is, our pride is pretty big and publicly known like it’s not a secret. But this overexposure of pride is starting to go out of control. At this point, saying “I’m pinoy” to a foreigner is like saying “I’m good at everything”, all because of our overexposure of pride.

Introduction Goal and Solutions

The goal is to make them understand that our pride is not a thing to be negative at. The solution of keeping our mouths shut to not reveal our pride out to the world if it is either authentic or hubristic is out of the window, since the problem already started. Some solutions I think would be great is to do a public post about it. A public post on social media could help in spreading the word, it would be better if it was a celebrity/influencer since their voices can be heard more. A movement might just be overreacting since this problem isn’t that big of a social problem. Another solution is to avoid making pride hubristic. We do have people here in the Philippines making filipino pride hubristic since they would celebrate other filipino victories when they did absolutely nothing. People who feel pride from other people’s victories, like Pacquiao, just because they’re filipino is hubristic pride.

All things considered, Pinoy pride is overly known to the point where people think it’s hubristic when it’s not. It feels better to have something to be proud of, like being Filipino, and in times of calamity, poverty and national insecurity, we all need something about which to feel good.
